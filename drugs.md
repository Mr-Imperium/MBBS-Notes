## A. ANTIBIOTICS (ANTIBACTERIAL)

### 1. **Fluoroquinolones**

**Drugs:** Moxifloxacin, Ciprofloxacin, Ofloxacin

**Mechanism of action:** Inhibit bacterial DNA gyrase (topoisomerase II) and topoisomerase IV → impaired DNA replication.

**Uses:**

* Bacterial conjunctivitis
* Keratitis, corneal ulcers
* Prophylaxis in ocular surgery

**Contraindications / Cautions:**

* Hypersensitivity to quinolones
* Relative caution in children (systemic use)

---

### 2. **Aminoglycosides**

**Drugs:** Gentamicin, Tobramycin

**Mechanism of action:** Bind 30S ribosomal subunit → inhibit protein synthesis (bactericidal).

**Uses:**

* Gram‑negative infections
* Bacterial conjunctivitis, blepharitis
* Severe infections (IV gentamicin)

**Contraindications / Cautions:**

* Hypersensitivity
* Prolonged use → epithelial toxicity
* Renal & ototoxicity (systemic)

---

### 3. **Combination Antibiotic + Steroid**

**Drugs:** Betamethasone + Neomycin

**Mechanism of action:**

* Neomycin: inhibits protein synthesis
* Betamethasone: suppresses inflammation via phospholipase‑A2 inhibition

**Uses:**

* Post‑operative inflammation with infection risk
* Severe allergic/inflammatory conditions

**Contraindications:**

* Viral keratitis (HSV)
* Fungal infections
* Glaucoma (long‑term)

---

## B. ANTIFUNGAL

### **Natamycin**

**Mechanism of action:** Binds ergosterol → disrupts fungal cell membrane.

**Uses:**

* Fungal keratitis (especially filamentous fungi)

**Contraindications:**

* Hypersensitivity

---

## C. ANTIVIRAL

### **Acyclovir**

**Mechanism of action:** Guanosine analogue → inhibits viral DNA polymerase.

**Uses:**

* Herpes simplex keratitis
* Herpes zoster ophthalmicus

**Contraindications / Cautions:**

* Renal impairment (systemic)

---

## D. MYDRIATICS & CYCLOPLEGICS

### 1. **Antimuscarinics**

**Drugs:** Atropine, Cyclopentolate, Tropicamide

**Mechanism of action:** Block muscarinic receptors → mydriasis & cycloplegia.

**Uses:**

* Refraction (cycloplegic)
* Uveitis (prevent synechiae)

**Contraindications:**

* Angle‑closure glaucoma
* Infants (atropine → toxicity)

---

### 2. **Sympathomimetic**

**Drug:** Phenylephrine

**Mechanism of action:** α‑1 agonist → pupillary dilatation (no cycloplegia).

**Uses:**

* Fundus examination

**Contraindications:**

* Hypertension, cardiac disease

---

## E. ANTI‑GLAUCOMA DRUGS

### 1. **Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors**

**Drugs:** Dorzolamide (topical), Acetazolamide (oral)

**Mechanism of action:** ↓ aqueous humor production.

**Uses:**

* Open‑angle glaucoma
* Acute angle‑closure (acetazolamide)

**Contraindications:**

* Sulfa allergy
* Renal disease

---

### 2. **Prostaglandin Analogues**

**Drug:** Bimatoprost

**Mechanism of action:** ↑ uveoscleral outflow.

**Uses:**

* Primary open‑angle glaucoma

**Contraindications / Cautions:**

* Uveitis
* Iris pigmentation changes

---

### 3. **Beta‑blockers**

**Drug:** Timolol

**Mechanism of action:** ↓ aqueous humor secretion.

**Uses:**

* Glaucoma

**Contraindications:**

* Asthma, COPD
* Heart block

---

### 4. **Alpha‑2 Agonist**

**Drug:** Brimonidine

**Mechanism of action:** ↓ aqueous production & ↑ uveoscleral outflow.

**Uses:**

* Glaucoma

**Contraindications:**

* Infants
* MAO‑inhibitor therapy

---

## F. ANTI‑INFLAMMATORY

### 1. **Steroids**

**Drugs:** Prednisolone acetate, Dexamethasone IV, Betamethasone

**Mechanism of action:** Inhibit inflammatory mediators.

**Uses:**

* Uveitis
* Post‑operative inflammation

**Contraindications:**

* Active infection
* Glaucoma

---

### 2. **NSAID**

**Drug:** Flurbiprofen

**Mechanism of action:** COX inhibition → ↓ prostaglandins.

**Uses:**

* Post‑operative pain
* Prevent miosis during surgery

**Contraindications:**

* Corneal epithelial defects

---

## G. LUBRICANTS & TEAR SUBSTITUTES

### **Carboxymethyl Cellulose**

**Mechanism:** Lubrication & tear film stabilization.

**Uses:**

* Dry eye syndrome

**Contraindications:**

* None significant

---

## H. ANTI‑ALLERGIC

### **Cromolyn Sodium**

**Mechanism of action:** Mast cell stabilizer → prevents histamine release.

**Uses:**

* Allergic conjunctivitis

**Contraindications:**

* Hypersensitivity

---

## I. DIAGNOSTIC AGENTS

### **Fluorescein Strip**

**Use:** Corneal epithelial defects, applanation tonometry

### **Schirmer Tear Test Strip**

**Use:** Tear secretion assessment

### **Trypan Blue**

**Use:** Capsulorhexis staining in cataract surgery

---

## J. MISCELLANEOUS

### **Lignocaine (Xylocaine)**

**Mechanism:** Sodium channel blockade → local anesthesia

**Uses:**

* Topical anesthesia
* Peribulbar block

**Contraindications:**

* Hypersensitivity

---

### **Hyaluronidase (IV / local)**

**Mechanism:** Breaks hyaluronic acid → enhances diffusion

**Uses:**

* Added to local anesthetic blocks

**Contraindications:**

* Hypersensitivity

---

### **Sodium Chloride (Normal / Hypertonic)**

**Uses:**

* Irrigation
* Corneal edema (hypertonic)
