Here are the best answers based on the standard frameworks found in **Kaplan & Sadock’s Synopsis of Psychiatry** (for clinical definitions, etiology, and criteria) and **K.D. Tripathi / Praveen Tripathi** (for pharmacological classifications and exam-oriented management).

---

### 1. Define Mood & Affect

*   **Mood:**
    *   **Definition:** A pervasive and sustained emotion that colors the person's perception of the world. It is the patient's *internal* emotional state (subjective).
    *   **Kaplan's Analogy:** Mood is like the "climate" (long-term).
    *   **Examples:** Depressed, euphoric, irritable, anxious.
*   **Affect:**
    *   **Definition:** The external expression of emotion attached to ideas or mental representations. It is the *observed* emotional responsiveness (objective).
    *   **Kaplan's Analogy:** Affect is like the "weather" (short-term/fluctuating).
    *   **Descriptors:** Blunted, flat, constricted, inappropriate, labile.

### 2. Depression: Etiology, Criteria & Treatment

**A. Etiology (Bio-Psycho-Social Model)**
*   **Biological:**
    *   **Neurotransmitters:** Monoamine hypothesis (Deficiency of Serotonin, Norepinephrine, Dopamine).
    *   **Neuroendocrine:** HPA axis hyperactivity (increased cortisol).
    *   **Genetics:** High concordance in monozygotic twins (~50%).
*   **Psychological:**
    *   **Freud:** Anger turned inward; loss of a love object.
    *   **Beck’s Cognitive Triad:** Negative view of Self, World, and Future.
*   **Social:** Stressful life events, unemployment, lack of social support.

**B. Diagnostic Criteria (DSM-5 Summary for Major Depressive Disorder)**
*   **Duration:** Symptoms present for at least **2 weeks**.
*   **Key Symptoms:** Must have at least one of: (1) Depressed mood or (2) Anhedonia (loss of interest).
*   **Total 5+ symptoms required** (SIG E CAPS):
    *   **S**leep disturbance (Insomnia/Hypersomnia)
    *   **I**nterest reduced (Anhedonia)
    *   **G**uilt or worthlessness
    *   **E**nergy loss (Fatigue)
    *   **C**oncentration impairment
    *   **A**ppetite changes (Weight loss/gain)
    *   **P**sychomotor agitation/retardation
    *   **S**uicidal ideation

**C. Treatment**
*   **Pharmacotherapy (Ref: K.D. Tripathi):**
    1.  **SSRIs (First Line):** Escitalopram, Sertraline, Fluoxetine.
    2.  **SNRIs:** Venlafaxine, Duloxetine.
    3.  **TCAs:** Amitriptyline, Imipramine (more side effects like dry mouth, constipation).
    4.  **MAO Inhibitors:** Moclobemide (rarely used now).
    5.  **Atypical:** Mirtazapine, Bupropion.
*   **Psychotherapy:** CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy), Interpersonal Therapy.
*   **Somatic Therapy:** ECT (Electroconvulsive Therapy) for severe/suicidal/catatonic cases.

### 3. Withdrawal Seizure vs. Independent Seizure (Alcohol)

| Feature | **Withdrawal Seizure (Rum Fits)** | **Independent Seizure (Epilepsy)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cause** | Abrupt cessation of alcohol (drop in blood alcohol level). | Underlying neurological condition (scarring, genetic). |
| **Timing** | Occurs **6–48 hours** after last drink. | Can occur anytime (unprovoked). |
| **Type** | Generalized Tonic-Clonic (GTCS). Rarely focal. | Can be focal or generalized. |
| **EEG** | Usually Normal (inter-ictal). | Often Abnormal (epileptiform discharges). |
| **Treatment** | **Benzodiazepines** (Diazepam/Lorazepam). No long-term AEDs needed. | **Anti-Epileptic Drugs (AEDs)** (e.g., Valproate, Phenytoin) long-term. |

### 4. Schizophrenia

**A. Definition:** A chronic psychotic disorder characterized by disturbances in thinking, emotion, and behavior, lasting >6 months.

**B. Etiology:**
*   **Dopamine Hypothesis:** Excess dopamine in mesolimbic pathway (positive symptoms) and deficit in mesocortical pathway (negative symptoms).
*   **Neurodevelopmental:** Ventricular enlargement, cortical atrophy.
*   **Genetics:** Chromosome 6, 8, 22 links.

**C. Schneider’s First Rank Symptoms (SFRS) - *Important for Exams*:**
1.  **Auditory Hallucinations:** Voices arguing, voices commenting on one's action, thoughts spoken aloud (Gedankenlautwerden).
2.  **Delusions of Thought:** Thought insertion, Thought withdrawal, Thought broadcasting.
3.  **Somatic Passivity:** Sensation of being touched or controlled by external forces.
4.  **Delusional Perception:** Normal perception given a bizarre personal meaning (e.g., "The traffic light turned red, so I knew I was the King").

**D. Negative Symptoms (The 5 A's):**
*   **Affect:** Flattened/blunted.
*   **Avolition:** Lack of motivation.
*   **Alogia:** Poverty of speech.
*   **Anhedonia:** Lack of pleasure.
*   **Asociality:** Social withdrawal.

**E. Good vs. Bad Prognosis**

| **Good Prognosis** | **Bad Prognosis** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Acute/Abrupt onset | Insidious/Gradual onset |
| Late onset (older age) | Early onset (young age) |
| Obvious precipitating factor | No precipitating factor |
| Positive symptoms dominant | Negative symptoms dominant |
| Good premorbid history | Poor premorbid history |
| Married, Good social support | Single/Divorced, Poor support |
| Mood symptoms present (Schizoaffective) | Family history of Schizophrenia |

**F. Management/Treatment:**
*   **Antipsychotics (K.D. Tripathi):**
    *   **Typical (1st Gen):** Haloperidol, Chlorpromazine (Good for positive symptoms, high risk of EPS).
    *   **Atypical (2nd Gen - First Line):** Risperidone, Olanzapine, Quetiapine, Clozapine (for resistant cases). *Mechanism: 5HT-2A & D2 antagonism.*
*   **Psychosocial:** Social skills training, Family therapy, Vocational rehabilitation.

### 5. Alcohol Dependence Syndrome (ADS)

**A. Etiology:** Genetic predisposition, reinforcing effects of alcohol (dopamine release in nucleus accumbens), cultural factors.

**B. Diagnostic Criteria (ICD-10/DSM-5):**
3 or more of the following in the last year:
1.  **Craving** (Strong desire to take the substance).
2.  **Control** (Difficulty controlling onset, termination, or levels of use).
3.  **Withdrawal** state upon cessation.
4.  **Tolerance** (Need for increased amounts).
5.  **Neglect** of alternative pleasures/interests.
6.  **Persistence** despite clear harmful consequences (liver damage, job loss).

**C. Management:**
1.  **Detoxification (Treating Withdrawal):**
    *   **Benzodiazepines:** Chlordiazepoxide or Diazepam (Gold standard to prevent seizures/Delirium Tremens).
    *   **Thiamine (Vit B1):** To prevent Wernicke’s Encephalopathy.
2.  **Maintenance (Anti-craving drugs - K.D. Tripathi):**
    *   **Acamprosate:** NMDA antagonist.
    *   **Naltrexone:** Opioid antagonist (reduces "high").
    *   **Disulfiram:** Aldehyde dehydrogenase inhibitor (Aversion therapy - causes vomiting if alcohol is consumed).

### 6. Short Notes

**A. PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder)**
*   **Definition:** Anxiety disorder following exposure to a traumatic event involving actual/threatened death or injury.
*   **Core Symptoms:**
    1.  **Re-experiencing:** Flashbacks, nightmares.
    2.  **Avoidance:** Avoiding places/thoughts related to trauma.
    3.  **Hyperarousal:** Hypervigilance, exaggerated startle response, insomnia.
*   **Duration:** Symptoms > 1 month.
*   **Treatment:** SSRIs (Sertraline, Paroxetine), Trauma-focused CBT, EMDR.

**B. GAD (Generalized Anxiety Disorder)**
*   **Definition:** Excessive, uncontrollable worry about multiple events (work, health, family) occurring more days than not for > 6 months.
*   **Symptoms:** "Worry wart," restlessness, fatigue, muscle tension, sleep disturbance.
*   **Treatment:** SSRIs, Buspirone (non-sedating anxiolytic), Benzodiazepines (short-term only), CBT.

**C. OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)**
*   **Obsessions:** Recurrent, intrusive thoughts/images that cause anxiety (e.g., contamination, doubt).
*   **Compulsions:** Repetitive behaviors performed to reduce the anxiety (e.g., washing, checking).
*   **Treatment:** High dose SSRIs (Fluoxetine, Fluvoxamine), Clomipramine (TCA), CBT (Exposure and Response Prevention - ERP).

**D. Mania**
*   **Definition:** Distinct period of abnormally elevated, expansive, or irritable mood lasting > 1 week (or requiring hospitalization).
*   **Symptoms (DIG FAST):** Distractibility, Indiscretion (risky behavior), Grandiosity, Flight of ideas, Activity increased, Sleep need decreased, Talkativeness.
*   **Treatment:** Mood Stabilizers (Lithium, Valproate), Antipsychotics for acute agitation.

**E. Postpartum Depression (PPD)**
*   **Timing:** Onset within 4 weeks of delivery (DSM-5), though can occur up to a year.
*   **Different from "Baby Blues":** Blues resolve in 10 days; PPD persists and impairs functioning.
*   **Risk:** Harm to infant (thoughts of hurting baby).
*   **Treatment:** SSRIs (Sertraline is often preferred in breastfeeding), Psychotherapy.

**F. Mania vs. Hypomania**

| **Feature** | **Mania** | **Hypomania** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Duration** | > 7 days (or hospitalization). | > 4 days. |
| **Severity** | Severe impairment in functioning. | No marked impairment; observable change. |
| **Psychosis** | May be present (delusions/hallucinations). | Absent. |
| **Hospitalization** | Often required. | Not required. |

**G. History of Schizophrenia**
*   **Benedict Morel (1852):** Coined *Demence Precoce* (premature dementia).
*   **Emil Kraepelin (1896):** Defined *Dementia Praecox* (chronic, deteriorating course distinct from manic-depressive illness).
*   **Eugen Bleuler (1911):** Coined term **"Schizophrenia"** (split mind). Described the 4 A's (Autism, Ambivalence, Affect, Associations).
*   **Kurt Schneider:** Described First Rank Symptoms (FRS) for diagnosis.

**H. Opiate Dependence Syndrome & Withdrawal**
*   **Etiology:** Use of Heroin, Morphine, Codeine. Rapid tolerance and severe physical dependence.
*   **Withdrawal Symptoms:**
    *   **Early:** Lacrimation (tearing), Rhinorrhea (runny nose), Yawning, Sweating.
    *   **Late:** Piloerection ("Gooseflesh" -> "Cold Turkey"), Dilated pupils, Diarrhea, Muscle aches, Insomnia, Tachycardia.
*   **Treatment (Ref K.D. Tripathi):**
    *   **Overdose:** **Naloxone** (IV).
    *   **Withdrawal Management:**
        *   **Clonidine:** Alpha-2 agonist (reduces autonomic symptoms like sweating/tachycardia).
        *   **Methadone/Buprenorphine:** Substitution therapy (tapering down).
    *   **Long-term Maintenance:** **Buprenorphine** (Partial agonist) or **Naltrexone** (Antagonist to prevent relapse).
